What is a Packet?

When you send data (like a message or file) over a network, it doesn't go all at once. Instead, it gets broken into small pieces called packets.

Think of it like: Breaking a long letter into multiple envelopes. Each envelope has the sender's address, receiver's address, and part of the message.

What is an IP Address?

An IP Address is like a home address for your computer on the network. Every device connected to a network has a unique IP address.

Example IP Addresses:
  • 192.168.1.1
  • 192.168.1.2
  • 10.0.0.5

What is a Router?

A router is a device that helps packets find their way across the network. It reads the destination address on each packet and sends it in the right direction.

Router's job: It's like a post office - it receives packets and sends them to the correct destination.

How Does Data Travel?

When you send data from one computer to another, here's what happens:

Step 1: Your computer creates packets with your IP address and the destination IP address
Step 2: Your home router receives the packets
Step 3: The router sends them to the internet
Step 4: The packets travel through the internet (might take different routes)
Step 5: They reach the destination router
Step 6: Finally, the destination computer receives and reads the packets

Key Terms:

Packet: A small chunk of data with header info and the actual data
IP Address: Unique identifier for each device on a network
Router: Device that directs packets to their destination
Network: Group of connected computers that can share data
