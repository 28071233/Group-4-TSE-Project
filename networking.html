How the Animation Works

Stage 1 - Prepare: Computer A creates a packet with the sender's IP (192.168.1.1) and destination IP (192.168.1.2), then adds the data to send.

Stage 2 - Router 1: The packet travels from Computer A to your home router, which reads the destination address and decides where to send it next.

Stage 3 - ISP: Router 1 forwards the packet to your Internet Service Provider's network for transmission across the internet.

Stage 4 - Internet: The packet travels through multiple routers and networks across the internet, potentially taking different paths than other packets.

Stage 5 - Router 2: The packet arrives at the destination network's router, which recognizes the IP address and forwards it to Computer B.

Stage 6 - Receive: Computer B receives the packet, verifies it, and reassembles any data that may have been split into multiple packets.

What You're Seeing

The 📦 packet (shown as a box icon) travels in a circular path to represent its journey through the network layers. The numbered circles (1-6) mark each stage of the journey. The colored boxes below the animation show which stage is currently active (blue), completed (dark blue), or pending (gray).
